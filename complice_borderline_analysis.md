## The Art. 6(3) escape hatch

Even under Scenario B, Art. 6(3) allows the provider to self-assess that
the system is NOT high-risk if it "does not pose a significant risk of harm."

A documented Art. 6(3) self-assessment would need to argue:
- Complice does not *determine* access to healthcare services
- Human review is always available
- The system explicitly defers serious cases to professionals
- No binding decisions are made by the system

This is the most legally prudent path for a solo developer:
**classify conservatively as High Risk, conduct the Art. 6(3) assessment,
document the reasoning, and proceed with deployment.**
